Ingredients

Pastry

  • 250 g plain flour
  • 25 g caster sugar
  • 2-3 tbsp milk
  • 125g butter, very cold and diced
  • 2 large egg yolks
  • Pinch of Salt
  • Medium sized pie tin

Filling

  • 315g red apples, peeled and diced
  • 1 stalk rhubarb, diced
  • 1 egg white, beaten
  • 45g soft brown sugar, plus extra for sprinkling
  • 2 tbsp caster sugar
  • 45g butter, sofented, plus extra for brushing
  • 1 shot spiced rum
  • 2 tsp cinnamon
  • 11 tsp nutmeg
  • 1/2 tsp allspice
  • 1 tsp vanilla essence
  • 1/4 cup pecans, chopped

Directions

  1. To make the pie crust, put the flour, sugar, salt, and butter into a food processor and blend until a breadcrumb consistency is reached.
  2. Add the egg yolk and whizz again for a moment, and finally the milk.
  3. Once the dough has formed, turn it out onto a lightly floured surface and work until it is smooth.
  4. Wrap in cling wrap and chill for half an hour minimum before using.
  5. Preheat your oven to 200C/400F and place the pie tin in to heat up.
  6. In a mixing bowl, combine all the filling ingredients except for the egg white and mix thoroughly.
  7. Dust your work surface with flour and roll out your pastry into a circle to fit your pie tin, leaving aside a small quantity of dough to make the top lattice. Fit the pastry to the tin, then spoon in the filling evenly. Sprinkle the top with brown sugar.
  8. Using a roller cutter or knife, cut out a lattice with the last of the dough and secure to the top of your pie. Brush all over with butter and the egg white to glaze.
  9. Bake for 30 minutes on the middle shelf. Leave to cool for 15 minutes before removing from the tin.
  10. Serve hot with a drizzle of custard or vanilla ice cream.
